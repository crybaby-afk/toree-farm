Vegetable Guide

Growing Premium Spinach with Confidence

March 15, 2024 By Toree Farm Experts

Spinach is one of the most rewarding crops for both kitchen gardens and commercial plots. Strong seedlings help you start with better root systems and more uniform growth.

Key Growing Tips

  • Use fertile, well-drained soil with a near-neutral pH.
  • Keep moisture consistent, especially after transplanting.
  • Harvest outer leaves first for a longer production cycle.
  • Provide light midday protection in very hot conditions.

Seasonal Planting

Broccoli from Seedling to Harvest

March 10, 2024 By Agricultural Specialists

Broccoli performs best when it starts as a healthy seedling and moves into prepared soil at the right stage. Temperature, moisture, and spacing matter a lot.

Success Factors

  • Aim for cool to moderate growing conditions.
  • Feed with balanced nutrients to support head formation.
  • Keep weeds down to reduce competition for moisture.
  • Harvest promptly once heads are compact and firm.

Pest Management

Cleaner Pest Control for Vegetable Gardens

March 5, 2024 By Farm Managers

Strong seedlings reduce stress, but field care still matters. A simple pest management routine can protect your crops without overcomplicating the season.

Natural Solutions

  • Inspect leaves weekly for early aphid and whitefly activity.
  • Use neem-based sprays where appropriate.
  • Improve spacing and airflow to reduce disease pressure.
  • Keep field edges and weeds under control.

Soil Health

Preparing Soil for Better Yields

February 28, 2024 By Soil Experts

Healthy soil gives seedlings the best chance to establish quickly. Good drainage, organic matter, and balanced nutrition are the foundation of every successful field.

Soil Preparation

  • Test the soil before making major fertilizer decisions.
  • Add compost or well-rotted organic matter.
  • Prevent standing water around newly transplanted seedlings.
  • Rotate crops to support long-term soil health.
